# AI writing detox

Good writing is invisible. If readers notice the writing style, it's distracting from the content. AI patterns are noticeable—they break trust.

## When to use

- Writing or editing any academic or scholarly content
- Drafting research papers, theses, or dissertations
- Creating grant proposals or funding applications
- Writing documentation or reports
- Reviewing any AI-assisted text before submission

## Banned words (delete or replace)

| Word | Why it's bad | Alternative |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| delve | AI signature word | explore, examine, look at |
| realm | Pretentious | area, field |
| tapestry | Purple prose | (delete entirely) |
| landscape | Corporate speak | situation, environment |
| leverage | Jargon | use |
| utilize | Jargon | use |
| robust | Meaningless | strong, reliable |
| seamless | Almost always false | smooth, easy |
| comprehensive | Rarely necessary | full, complete |
| cutting-edge | Cliché | modern, new |
| holistic | Corporate | complete, full |
| synergy | Corporate | (delete, rewrite) |
| paradigm | Overused | model, approach |
| empower | Vague | (be specific about what capability) |
| innovative | Empty praise | (describe what's new) |
| transformative | Hyperbolic | changed, improved |
| rich | (as modifier) | (delete or be specific) |

## Banned phrases

### Throat-clearing (delete entirely)

- "It's important to note that..."
- "In today's [X] landscape..."
- "Let's dive/delve into..."
- "Without further ado..."
- "In this article, we will..."
- "It's worth mentioning that..."
- "It goes without saying..."
- "As we all know..."

### Empty hedges

- "To be fair..." / "To be honest..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "When it comes to..."
- "In terms of..."
- "With respect to..."

### AI enthusiasm

- "This is a game-changer"
- "...and that's a good thing!"
- "Here's the thing:"
- "...and that's okay!"
- "...and I'm here for it"

### Corporate buzzwords

- "Moving forward..."
- "Going forward..."
- "At this point in time..."
- "Due to the fact that..."
- "In order to..."

## Banned structures

### Don't start sentences with:

- "So," or "Well,"
- "Now," (when not about time)
- "Look," or "Listen,"
- "Basically," or "Essentially,"

### Don't end sentences with:

- "...right?"
- "...you know?"
- "...if you will"

## Case sensitivity

**Always use sentence case for headings, not title case.**

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| Getting Started With Your Project | Getting started with your project |
| How To Use The Tool | How to use the tool |
| Best Practices For Academic Writing | Best practices for academic writing |

## Academic-specific patterns to avoid

### "Not just X—it's Y" pattern

❌ "This isn't just a finding—it's a paradigm shift"
❌ "This wasn't just a study—it was a turning point"

✅ State the finding directly without dramatic framing

### "Fundamentally transforms" pattern

❌ "This fundamentally transforms how researchers approach X"
❌ "This represents a fundamental shift in understanding Y"

✅ Describe the actual contribution without hyperbole

### Inflated claims in academic writing

❌ "A groundbreaking study"
❌ "A major milestone in the field"
❌ "Unprecedented findings"

✅ Let the data speak. Reviewers judge significance.

### Empty transitions

❌ "With that in mind..."
❌ "Building on this foundation..."
❌ "Taking this a step further..."

✅ Just make the next point

## Before and after examples

### Example 1: Introduction

**AI slop:**
> In today's rapidly evolving research landscape, it's crucial to understand the multifaceted nature of cognitive biases. Let's delve into how these robust phenomena can fundamentally transform our understanding of decision-making.

**Human writing:**
> Cognitive biases distort judgment in predictable ways. Three are particularly well-documented in medical decision-making.

### Example 2: Methods paragraph

**AI slop:**
> A comprehensive and holistic approach was utilized to investigate the phenomenon, leveraging cutting-edge methodologies to ensure robust and seamless data collection across all participants.

**Human writing:**
> Participants completed a structured interview and two validated questionnaires over two sessions.

### Example 3: Discussion opener

**AI slop:**
> The findings from this study are truly transformative and represent a paradigm shift in how we conceptualize the rich tapestry of human social behavior in digital environments.

**Human writing:**
> These results contradict the social facilitation hypothesis. When observers were anonymous, performance declined rather than improved.

### Example 4: Conclusion

**AI slop:**
> Moving forward, stakeholders must leverage innovative approaches to navigate the challenging landscape of this transformative field and empower future researchers to holistically address these critical issues.

**Human writing:**
> Future studies should test whether the effect holds in non-Western populations, where social comparison norms differ.

## Red flags in academic writing

1. **Lists of near-synonyms**: "comprehensive, sophisticated, and robust" (pick one or none)
2. **Excessive hedging**: "may potentially be able to possibly..."
3. **Noun stacking**: "evidence-based methodology implementation framework"
4. **Passive voice hiding agency**: "It was determined that..." (by whom?)
5. **Circular definitions**: "The analysis enables analysis of the analytical results"

## Quick self-check before submitting

Search your text for:
1. "delve" → delete or replace
2. "landscape" → delete or replace
3. "crucial" → is it actually crucial?
4. "robust" → what does this mean here?
5. "leverage" → just say "use"
6. "comprehensive" → is this word doing any work?
7. Starting "So," → delete

Found any? Your writing needs another pass.

## The ultimate test

Before accepting any AI-assisted text, ask:

1. Can I delete this word/phrase without losing meaning? → Delete it
2. Is this the simplest way to say this? → Simplify
3. Would I say this out loud to a colleague? → If not, rewrite
4. Does this add information or just sound impressive? → If the latter, cut it

**Remember:** If reviewers think "AI wrote this," you've already lost their credibility.
